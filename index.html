Shuai Li

I am a tenure-track assistant professor in John Hopcroft Center of Shanghai Jiao Tong University.

I received my PhD degree in the Chinese University of Hong Kong under the supervision of Prof. Kwong-Sak Leung. Before that, I obtained my bachelor degree in Mathematics from Zhejiang University and my master degree in Mathematics from University of the Chinese Academy of Sciences.

My research interest lies on multi-armed bandits, online learning, statistical learning, reinforcement learning, machine learning and bioinformatics. I am looking forward to research collaborations with industry and academia. Please contact me if you are interested.

Prospective students: I am looking for outstanding and highly motivated students to work together bandits, machine learning theory and bioinformatics. Please email me with your CV and transcripts.

News

  • 09/2019: We have a new paper on the Gambler's problem. The work is accepted at the workshop of OptRL in NeurIPS 2019.

  • 08/2019: Here is the slides for my thesis defense.

  • 02/2019: We have a new paper of Improved Algorithm on Online Clustering of Bandits, which generalizes to allow users with different appearing probabilities and uses sets to efficiently split and merge user clusters. The work is accepted at IJCAI 2019. Another paper is the first work on Stochastic Online Learning with Probabilistic Graph Feedback, which considers both one-step and cascade feedback.

  • 10/2018: It is my honor to receive Google PhD Fellowship 2018 in the field of machine learning.

  • 10/2018: Tor, Csaba and I have a new paper on Online Learning to Rank with Features, which extends the existing setting for general click models to deal with a large number of items and the expected reward is a linear function on item feature vectors. We bring up a general click model with only a few assumptions and design a simple but efficient algorithm. The work is accepted at ICML 2019.

  • 2018.4.30 - 2018.8.31: I am a research intern at DeepMind working together with Csaba Szepesvari and Tor Lattimore on the problem of online learning to rank.

  • 2017.6.5 - 2017.11.10: I am a research intern at Adobe Research working together with Branislav Kveton on the problem of offline evaluation. The work is accepted at KDD 2018.
